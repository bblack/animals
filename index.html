    Ski Info and Checklist

    Saturday after the wedding some people are planning to go to the mountains to ski or snowboard. If the weather permits we will be driving to Breckenridge, which offers a variety of lessons for adults and children. It is about an hour and a half outside the city. Check out the pricing and details below. Lessons start EARLY so you will have to be up and out the door by 6:30am on Saturday to make the 9:30am all day lesson. If you don’t want to ski or snowboard there are other things to do in the town of Breckenridge plus the drive is gorgeous. Below is a checklist on what you need to ski or snowboard with some links. You can try to ski in your blue jeans, but you will be wet and freezing after your first fall. Also keep in mind there are other fees like lift and parking that I am not detailing. Gear:

  • Thermal, Water Wicking long sleeved shirt
  • Thermal, Water Wicking pants
  • Ski Socks - These are padded at the shin where a lot of pressure happens.
  • Snow jacket
  • Ski pants
  • Goggles - it is incredibly bright on the mountain so these or sunglasses are a must.
  • Gloves
    • You can often rent the gear. I found a place that rents the last four in the list as a package for $39/day, so you would only need to buy the thermals and socks. Rent Ski Clothes Here - You can have it delivered to your hotel for a small fee or take the 20 minute drive to Golden and pick it up for free. (I recommend picking them up the day before) Rent Skis and Snowboards Here - It will be for the “Breck” resort and you can pick up and drop off same day March 14th. Checkout Lesson Details Here - Pretty pricey, extremely early, but kinda necessary. I have a feeling we’ll all be feeling it that morning.
